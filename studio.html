The Studio

An architecture practice from Dhaka, since 2016.

Sthiti Limited is a Bangladesh-based architecture studio founded by Tajbir Ahmed Khan Rupok on 16 December 2016 under the name Sthiti Sthapotto, and incorporated as Sthiti Limited in December 2025. Across ten years of practice the studio has held its founding line of authority unbroken — one principal architect, one chief engineer, one chain of judgment from brief to handover.

Origin

Founded on a civic commission.

The studio began with a single brief — the Bangladesh Police Liberation War Museum at Rajarbag. The work was delivered four days ahead of schedule and inaugurated in 2017. That founding commission set the studio’s discipline: civic intent, technical rigour, and a refusal to dilute design responsibility through outsourcing.

Sthiti grew from that commission into a multi-discipline practice working across architecture, masterplan, landscape, interior, and construction management. The studio takes the project from first site visit through handover, with senior architects involved at every stage.

Principles

What the studio holds.

Four convictions shape every project Sthiti takes on.

Site First

No project starts with a style. It starts with climate, orientation, neighbours, soil and brief.

One Authority

One principal architect, one chief engineer. No project is quietly handed to a junior to finish.

Built Lines

The drawing line and the built line are the same line. Construction management stays in-studio.

Civic Tone

Even a private residence carries civic responsibility — to its street, its neighbours and its city.

Timeline

A decade of practice.

16 December 2016
Tajbir Ahmed Khan Rupok founded Sthiti Sthapotto and initiated the practice with Nazmus Sakib and Saiful Islam Raihan.
23 January 2017
Bangladesh Police Liberation War Museum at Rajarbag inaugurated. Sthiti completed the design and construction within 51 days — one month and twenty days from start to handover.
January 2018
Sthiti began the design masterplanning of the BEPZA Economic Zone, Mirsarai, as a sub-consultant of JPZ Bangladesh (PVT) Ltd. This year, Sthiti also proposed the Masterplan for BARD, Comilla, and completed numerous exterior and interior projects.
2019
Proposed a masterplan for Netrokona University, Netrokona. Sthiti took first place in the design presentation, with the extraordinary conceptual presentation by Architect Farah Chowdhury, where Tehami Hasan also contributed his presentation skills, and the full university’s psychological philosophy was guided by philosopher Mohammad Saleh Rabbi Jyoti. This year, Sthiti also proposed a Masterplan for Comilla University Phase 2, in association with JPZ Bangladesh (PVT) Ltd.
2020
Designed the Head Office for OMEGA Ltd at Panthapath, Dhaka.
2022
Mazhar Uddin Chowdhury Prince joined Sthiti as Partner and Director.
2023
Mazhar Uddin Chowdhury Prince awarded by the Institute of Architects Bangladesh (IAB) for his PMG Gallery project at his previous office, DWM4 Internds. From April 2023 to November 2024, Sthiti designed and constructed the Vacation House at Narail. The studio delivered a significant volume of interior and exterior projects, building a reputation for design with efficiency within budget.
December 2025
Sthiti Sthapotto incorporated into Sthiti Limited.
2026
Sthiti Limited is expanding its multidisciplinary venture across architecture, masterplan, landscape, interior and construction management.
